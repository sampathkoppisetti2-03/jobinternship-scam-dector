Money & Payment Demands

CRITICAL
💰

Asking for Money Upfront

Any request for payment before employment starts — registration, training, uniform, ID card, or background check fees. Legitimate employers NEVER charge candidates.

"registration fee" "training deposit" "security amount"
🏦

Requesting Bank Account Details

Asking for account number, IFSC, or UPI ID before you've signed any employment contract or even received a formal offer letter.

"salary setup" "advance payment" "payroll registration"
🔑

"Refundable" Security Deposits

Promises of a "100% refundable" deposit to "confirm your seat" or "block your position." Once paid, contact disappears entirely.

"fully refundable" "block your slot"
📦

Purchase a Starter Kit

WFH job offers that require buying a "starter pack", "equipment kit", or "data CD" to begin work. These kits are worthless and the job doesn't exist.

"work kit" "packing material"
📱

Suspicious Communication

CRITICAL
📧

Suspicious Email Domains

Emails from Gmail/Yahoo/Hotmail claiming to be from major companies. Real companies always use official corporate domains (e.g. @tcs.com, not @tcs-careers.gmail.com).

@gmail.com "HR" @yahoo.com "Recruiter" company-hr@gmail
💬

WhatsApp-Only Hiring

The entire recruitment process — interview, selection, offer — happens over WhatsApp or Telegram with no official email, company website visit, or video call.

"interview on WhatsApp" "join our group"
⚡

Urgent Hiring Pressure

"Last 2 seats left!" or "Decision needed in 2 hours!" — artificial urgency is a classic manipulation tactic to prevent you from thinking critically or verifying the offer.

"limited seats" "immediate joiners" "today only"
🌐

Suspicious/Misspelled Domains

URLs like "infosys-careers.xyz", "tcs-hiring.online", or "amazon-jobs.tk" — typosquatted domains designed to look legitimate at first glance.

.xyz domains .tk / .ml sites company-name.online
📄

Offer Letter & Job Description

WARNING
💎

Unrealistically High Salary

Offers of ₹40,000–₹80,000/month for freshers with "no experience required" or generic roles like "data entry operator" or "form filler." Real entry-level salaries are far more modest.

"₹50K from Day 1" "no experience needed"
❓

Vague Job Description

Offers with no specific role title, no defined responsibilities, or generic descriptions like "online work", "social media tasks", or "computer work from home."

"online work" "computer work" "easy tasks"
🪪

Asking for Aadhaar / PAN Upfront

Requesting KYC documents like Aadhaar, PAN, or passport before you've even confirmed joining. These are used for identity fraud and opening fraudulent accounts.

"submit Aadhaar for verification" "KYC mandatory"
📋

Offer Letter with Grammar Errors

Poorly formatted offer letters with spelling mistakes, inconsistent fonts, wrong company addresses, or missing details like CIN/TAN numbers that all registered companies have.

No CIN number Wrong letterhead Typos in company name
✅

Your Safety Verification Checklist

DO THIS
🔍

Verify Company on MCA Website

Check the company's CIN on mca.gov.in to confirm it's a registered legal entity in India.

📞

Call the Company Directly

Find the official phone number from the company's real website (not from the email) and call to verify the job posting.

🌐

Check the Email Domain

The recruiter's email domain should exactly match the company's official website domain. No exceptions.

💳

Never Pay for a Job

Any payment request — regardless of the reason — is an automatic disqualifier. Report it to cybercrime.gov.in.
